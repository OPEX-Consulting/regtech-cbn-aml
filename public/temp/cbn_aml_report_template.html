Section One

Executive Summary

GG operates in one of the highest-risk payment corridors the CBN regulates. As an IMTO with cross-border exposure and a self-reported material fraud history, the institution enters this assessment carrying the full weight of the CBN's expectations — and very little of the infrastructure to meet them.

The CBN Automated AML Baseline Standards (Circular BSD/DIR/PUB/LAB/019/002) were issued on 10 March 2026. They represent the most comprehensive and enforceable AML technology standard the Central Bank has ever published for Nigerian financial institutions. The circular does not merely recommend good practice — it defines, with precision, the minimum functional architecture that every covered institution must demonstrate during examination. Institutions that cannot produce contemporaneous evidence against each of the 12 standards face supervisory action regardless of intent or good faith.

This report reflects GG's current compliance position as at April 2026, assessed against all 12 Baseline Standards and the cross-cutting Section 6 governance obligations. The findings are direct, not diplomatic. GG has no integrated AML system. It has no immutable audit trail, no automated transaction monitoring, no real-time sanctions screening, and no case management infrastructure. Of the 12 standards, 10 carry a Critical Gap rating. Two carry a Gap Identified rating. None are assessed as Compliant.

This is not an unusual position for an IMTO of GG's size and profile — but the urgency is real. A mandatory roadmap submission to the CBN Compliance Department is due on 10 June 2026. That deadline is not conditional on compliance status. Every institution must submit. An absent or inadequate submission is itself a finding. OPEX Consulting can complete that submission on GG's behalf, using the findings in this report, within days.

⚠ The CBN has explicitly stated that AML solutions without effective linkage to CDD/KYC/KYB information and customer risk assessments will not be regarded as compliant. Standalone tools, batch-feed monitoring, and manual processes do not satisfy the Baseline Standards — regardless of how long they have been in use.

Section Six

Top 5 Priority Actions Before 10 June 2026

The five actions below represent the minimum GG must take to submit a credible, CBN-compliant roadmap by the 10 June 2026 deadline. Credibility matters — the CBN does not assess roadmaps on a pass/fail basis. It assesses whether the institution's plan is specific, evidence-based, and proportionate to its risk profile. A generic or incomplete submission is treated as a finding.

01
Complete and Submit the CBN Implementation Roadmap Template
Hard Deadline: 10 June 2026 · Mandatory for All Institutions

The CBN AML Baseline Standards Implementation Roadmap Template — issued alongside Circular BSD/DIR/PUB/LAB/019/002 — must be completed and submitted to the CBN Compliance Department by 10 June 2026. The template spans 12 sections: executive summary, institution profile, gap analysis, implementation strategy, platform selection rationale, phased timeline, resource plan, governance framework, risk appetite statement, training plan, and Board sign-off. Every field must be completed. Blank, generic, or unsupported responses are treated as non-compliance.

This is not a document GG should complete alone. An incorrectly structured or inadequately evidenced submission can create a worse impression than a delayed one. OPEX Consulting can complete the template on GG's behalf using the findings from this report, typically within 5–7 business days of engagement, and can obtain Board approval support as part of that service.

Select an AML Platform and Document the Implementation Approach
Recommended by: May 2026 · Mandatory for Roadmap Credibility

Without a platform selection, the roadmap cannot be credibly completed — and a roadmap that names no platform is a roadmap that names no plan.

Obtain Board Approval for a Revised AML/CFT/CPF Policy
Recommended by: May 2026 · Mandatory for Section 6 Compliance

GG has a Board-approved AML/CFT/CPF policy in place — this is one of only two governance controls currently confirmed. Implement Real-Time Sanctions and PEP Screening Immediately

The CBN requires real-time screening with AI/fuzzy name matching against domestic and global lists — manual list checks or periodic batch screening do not satisfy this standard.

Screening can often be deployed ahead of the full AML platform implementation — it does not require the entire system to be live. This is a governance finding in its own right — it does not require a technology gap to be a problem. The institution must either extend its internal audit function to cover AML or engage an external co-source to conduct an AML-specific audit.
